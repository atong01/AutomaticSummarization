A '''declaration of independence''' or '''declaration of statehood''' is an assertion by a defined territory that it is [[independence|independent]] and constitutes a [[Sovereign state|state]]. Such places are usually declared from part or all of the [[wiktionary:Territory|territory]] of another nation or failed nation, or are breakaway territories from within the larger state. In 2010, the UN's [[International Court of Justice]] ruled in [[International Court of Justice advisory opinion on Kosovo's declaration of independence|an advisory opinion in Kosovo]] that "International law contains no prohibition on declarations of independence",<ref name="International Court of Justice">{{cite web|url=http://www.icj-cij.org/docket/files/141/15987.pdf |title=Accordance with International Law of the Unilateral Declaration of Independence in Respect of Kosovo |publisher=Icj-cij.org |accessdate=2014-02-14}}</ref> though the state from which the territory wishes to secede may regard the declaration as [[rebellion]], which may lead to a [[war of independence]] or a constitutional settlement to resolve the crisis. Not all declarations of independence succeed in the formation of an independent state.
