==Population==
According to the preliminary 2011 census results [[Dominica]] has a population of 71,293.<ref name="2011 census"/> The population growth rate is very low, due primarily to emigration to more prosperous Caribbean Islands, the [[United Kingdom]], the [[United States]], and [[Canada]]. The estimated mid-year population of 2014 is 72,300 (medium fertility scenario of The 2012 Revision of the World Population Prospects).<ref name="WPP 2012">[http://esa.un.org/wpp/index.htm Population Division of the Department of Economic and Social Affairs of the United Nations Secretariat, World Population Prospects: The 2012 Revision]</ref>

==Ethnic groups==
The vast majority of Dominicans are of African descent (75% at the 2014 census).<ref name="2001 census">http://www.caricomstats.org/Files/Publications/NCR%20Reports/Dominica.pdf</ref> There is a significant mixed population (19%)  at the 2014 census due to intermarriage, along with a small European origin minority (0.8%; descendants of French, British, and Irish colonists), East Indians (0.1%) groups, and there are small numbers of Lebanese/Syrians (0.1%) and Asians.

===[[Indigenous peoples of the Americas|Amerindians]]===
[[Dominica]] is the only Eastern Caribbean island that still has a population of pre-Columbian native [[Island Caribs|Caribs]] (also known as [[Island Caribs|Kalinago]]), who were exterminated or driven from neighbouring islands. According to the 2001 census there are only 2,001 [[Island Caribs|Caribs]] remaining (2.9% of the total population). A considerable growth occurred since the 1991 census when 1,634 [[Island Caribs|Caribs]] were counted (2.4% of the total population). 
The [[Island Caribs|Caribs]] live in eight villages on the east coast of [[Dominica]]. This special [[Carib Territory]] was granted by the British Crown in 1903.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.avirtualdominica.com/caribs.htm |title=The Carib Indians |publisher=Avirtualdominica.com |date= |accessdate=2010-06-27}}</ref>
The present number of [[Island Caribs|Kalinago]] is estimated at 4% more than 3,000.<ref>[http://kalinagoterritory.com] Kalinago Territory</ref>

==Languages==
[[English language|English]] is the official language and universally understood; however, because of historic [[French language|French]] domination, [[Antillean Creole]], a French-lexified creole language, is also widely spoken.

==Religion==
{{main|Religion in Dominica}}
According to the 2001 census, 91.2% percent of the population of [[Dominica]] is considered [[Christianity|Christian]], 1.6% has a non-Christian religion and 6.1% has no religion or did not state a religion (1.1%).<ref name="2001 census"/>

Roughly two thirds of [[Christianity|Christians]] are [[Roman Catholics]] (61.4% of the total population), a reflection of early French influence on the island, and one third are [[Protestant]]. The [[Evangelicalism|Evangelicals]] constitute the largest Protestant group, with 6.7% of the population. [[Seventh-day Adventists]] are the second largest group (6.1%). The next largest group are [[Pentecostals]] (5.6% of the population), followed by [[Baptists]] (4.1%).  Other [[Christianity|Christians]] include [[Methodists#Caribbean|Methodists]] (3.7%), Church of God (1.2%), [[Jehovah's Witnesses]] (1.2%), [[Anglicanism]] (0.6%) and [[Brethren (disambiguation)|Brethren]] (0.3%).
During the past decades the number of [[Roman Catholics]] and [[Anglicans]] has decreased, while the number of other Protestants has increased, especially [[Evangelicalism|Evangelicals]], [[Seventh-day Adventists]], [[Pentecostals]] (5.6% of the population) and [[Baptists]]).<ref name="2001 census"/>

The number of non-Christians is small. These religious groups include the [[Rastafarian Movement]] (1.3% of the population), [[Hinduism in the West Indies|Hinduism]] (0.1%) and [[Muslims]] (0.2%).
