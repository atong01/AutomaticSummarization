The '''Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe Party''' ('''ALDE Party''') is a [[European political party]] mainly active in the [[European Union]], composed of 60 national-level [[Liberalism|liberal]] parties from across Europe. Until 10 November 2012, the party was known as '''European Liberal Democrat and Reform Party''' (ELDR).<ref>{{cite web|author=|url=http://www.eldr.eu/en/press-releases/european-liberal-democrats-change-party-name-alde-party-0 |title=European Liberal Democrats change party name to ALDE Party &#124; ALDE Party |publisher=Eldr.eu |date= |accessdate=2013-09-23}}</ref> The ALDE Party is affiliated with the [[Liberal International]].<ref>http://www.liberal-international.org/site/Co-operating_Organsisations.html</ref>

Having developed from a loose confederation of national political parties in the 1970s, the ALDE Party is a recognised European political party incorporated as a non-profit association under [[law of Belgium|Belgian law]].

{{as of|2015}}, ALDE is the third largest European-wide political party represented in European Union institutions, with 70 [[Member of the European Parliament|MEPs]] and 5 members of the [[European Commission]]. Of the 28 [[member states of the European Union|EU member states]], there are seven with ALDE-affiliated Prime Ministers: [[Xavier Bettel]] ([[Democratic Party (Luxembourg)|DP)]] in [[Luxembourg]], [[Charles Michel]] ([[Mouvement Réformateur|MR]]) in [[Belgium]], [[Taavi Rõivas]] ([[Estonian Reform Party|RE]]) in [[Estonia]], [[Miro Cerar]] ([[Stranka Mira Cerarja|SMC]]) in [[Slovenia]], [[Juha Sipilä]] ([[Centre Party (Finland)|KESK]]) in [[Finland]], [[Mark Rutte]] ([[People's Party for Freedom and Democracy|VVD]]) in the [[Netherlands]] and [[Lars Løkke Rasmussen]] ([[Venstre (Denmark)|Venstre]]) in [[Denmark]]. Liberals are also in government in three other EU member states: [[Croatia]], [[Czech Republic]] and [[Lithuania]].

Since 20 July 2004, the ALDE Party is politically represented in the [[European Parliament]] by the [[Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe Group|Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe]] (ALDE) [[Political groups of the European Parliament|parliamentary group]], formed in conjunction with the [[European Democratic Party]] (EDP). The ALDE parliamentary group is led by [[Guy Verhofstadt]], a former [[Prime Minister of Belgium]]. Prior to the [[European Parliament election, 2004|2004 European election]] the party was attached to the [[European Liberal Democrat and Reform Party (European Parliament group)|European Liberal Democrat and Reform Party]] (ELDR) Group.

The youth wing of ALDE is the [[European Liberal Youth]] (LYMEC), which is predominantly based upon youth and student liberal organisations but contains also a small number of individual members. LYMEC is led by Vedrana Gujic ([[Croatian People's Party – Liberal Democrats|HNS]], Croatia), who was elected for a two-year term as LYMEC President in May 2014, and counts 200,000 members.

== History of pan-European liberalism ==
{{main|History of pan-European liberalism}}
[[File:ELDR logo.svg|thumb|150px|float|right|ELDR Party logo (2009-2012).]]

Pan-European liberalism has a long history dating back to the foundation of Liberal International in April 1947. In March 1976, the Federation of Liberal and Democrat Parties in Europe was established. The founding parties of the federation were the [[Free Democratic Party (Germany)|Free Democratic Party]] of Germany, [[Radical Party (France)|Radical Party]] of France, [[Liberal Party (Denmark)|Liberal Party]] of Denmark, [[Italian Liberal Party]], Dutch [[People's Party for Freedom and Democracy]] and [[Democratic Party (Luxembourg)|Democratic Party]] of Luxembourg.<ref name="Almeida2012">{{cite book|author=Dimitri Almeida|title=The Impact of European Integration on Political Parties: Beyond the Permissive Consensus|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=oD7bKbo0FYEC&pg=PA102|year=2012|publisher=Taylor & Francis|isbn=978-1-136-34039-0|pages=102–103}}</ref> Observer members joining later in 1976 were the [[Danish Social Liberal Party]], French [[Radical Party of the Left]] and [[Independent Republicans]], British [[Liberal Party (UK)|Liberal Party]], and [[Italian Republican Party]].<ref name="Almeida2012"/> The federation gradually evolved into the European Liberal Democrat and Reform Party (ELDR) with a matching group in the European Parliament, the [[European Liberal Democrat and Reform Party (European Parliament group)|European Liberal Democrat and Reform Party]] Group.

At an extraordinary Congress in Brussels held on 30 April 2004 the day before the [[enlargement of the European Union]], the ELDR Party incorporated itself under Belgian law and became a [[European political party]].

The ELDR Party allied with the [[European Democratic Party]] in 2004 to form the [[Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe]] (ALDE), with a matching [[Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe Group|ALDE Group in the European Parliament]]. The ELDR Party adopted its current name on 10 November 2012 in order to match the pan-European alliance and parliamentary group.
