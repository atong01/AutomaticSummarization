==As a khagan==
In 716, [[Kapaghan Khagan]] the second khaghan of the khaganate was killed in his campaign against [[Tokuz-Oguzes|Toquz Oghuz]] and his severed head was sent to [[Chang'an]].<ref name="Tang194">''[[Old Book of Tang]]'', [[:zh:s:èˆŠå”æ›¸/å·194ä¸Š|Vol. 194-I]]</ref> Although his son [[Inel Qaghan]] succeeded him, the legal claimant of the throne was his cousin AshÇnÃ  mÃ²jÃ­liÃ¡n. Mojilan's brother [[Kul Tigin]] and [[Tonyukuk]] carried out a [[coup d'Ã©tat]] against Inel Qaghan. They killed Inel Qaghan and made Mojilan a khagan with the name Bilge Qaghan.<ref name="Tang194"/> His name literally means "wise king".

Bilge's khaganate spanned vast steppes from the [[Caspian Sea]] to [[Manchuria]]; he also invaded the western sections of the [[China|Chinese]] territories. After his death from poisoning, several [[stela]]e were erected in [[Orkhon Valley|the capital area]] by the [[Orkhon River]]. These [[Orkhon inscriptions]] are the first known texts in the [[Old Turkic language]].
