The '''demographics of Ethiopia''' encompass the demographic features of [[Ethiopia]]'s inhabitants, including ethnicity, languages, population density, education level, health, economic status, religious affiliations and other aspects of the population.

==Ethnic groups==
{{main|List of ethnic groups in Ethiopia}}
The country's population is highly diverse, containing over 80 different ethnic groups. Most people in Ethiopia speak [[Afro-Asiatic languages]], mainly of the [[Semitic languages|Semitic]] or [[Cushitic languages|Cushitic]] branches. The latter include the [[Oromo people|Oromo]], [[Amhara people|Amhara]], [[Tigray-Tigrinya people|Tigray]] and [[Somali people|Somali]], which together make up three-quarters of the population.

[[Nilo-Saharan languages|Nilo-Saharan]]-speaking [[Nilotic]] ethnic minorities also inhabit the southern regions of the country, particularly in areas bordering [[South Sudan]]. Among these are the [[Mursi people|Mursi]] and [[Anuak people|Anuak]].

==Languages==
{{main|Languages of Ethiopia}}
There are 90 individual languages of Ethiopia according to [[Ethnologue]],<ref name="Ethnologue">[http://www.ethnologue.com/show_country.asp?name=ET ''Ethnologue'' page on Ethiopian languages]</ref> with the 1994 Ethiopian census indicating that some 77 tongues were spoken locally. Most of these languages belong to the [[Afroasiatic languages|Afro-Asiatic]] family ([[Cushitic languages|Cushitic]] and [[Semitic languages|Semitic]]). [[Omotic languages]] are also spoken, though their classification is uncertain. Additionally, [[Nilo-Saharan languages]] are spoken by the nation's [[Nilotic]] ethnic minorities.

[[Amharic language|Amharic]] 25.7% (as a first language), [[Oromo language|Afaan Oromo]] 31.6%, [[Somali language|Somali]] 6.5%, [[Tigrinya language|Tigrinya]] 6.1%, [[Gurage languages|Guragigna]] 3.5%, [[Sidamo language|Sidamo]] 3.5%, other local languages; [[Arabic language|Arabic]], [[English language|English]] (major foreign language taught in schools),<ref>(?The is nothing stated on the reference if 32.7% speakers of Amharic Language speak it as a first language-Additional reference please or correction please)The World Factbook, [https://www.cia.gov/library/publications/the-world-factbook/geos/et.html "Ethiopia."] 2006. (The preliminary draft of the 2007 national census does not provide figures on language speakers.)</ref>

Amharic is the only official national language although Afaan Oromo enjoys almost equal number of native speakers. It was also the language of primary school instruction, but has been replaced in many areas by local languages such as Afaan Oromo and Tigrinya. [[English language|English]] is the most widely spoken foreign language and is taught in all secondary schools.

==Religions==
{{main|Religion in Ethiopia}}
Ethiopia is a multi-religious country. Most of the [[Christian]]s live in the [[Ethiopian highlands|highlands]], while the [[Muslim]]s mainly inhabit the [[lowland]]s. Adherents of [[Traditional African religions|traditional faiths]] are primarily concentrated in the southern regions.

[[Ethiopian Orthodox]] 43.5%, [[Protestantism|Protestant]] 18.6% (which include [[P'ent'ay]] and the [[Ethiopian Evangelical Church Mekane Yesus]]), [[Islam in Ethiopia|Muslim]] 33.9%, traditional (2.6%) [[Roman Catholicism in Ethiopia|Catholic]] 0.7%, all others 0.6%.<ref name=Census-2007>[http://www.csa.gov.et/pdf/Cen2007_firstdraft.pdf 2007 Ethiopian census, first draft], Ethiopian Central Statistical Agency (accessed 6 May 2009) {{wayback|url=http://www.csa.gov.et/pdf/Cen2007_firstdraft.pdf |date=20120214221803 }}</ref> A small [[Ethiopian Jewish]] community also reside in the northern parts of the country although almost all of them have emigrated to [[Israel]].{{citation needed|date=January 2013}}

===UN estimates===
According to the 2010 revision of the UN's World Population Prospects, the total population was 82,950,000 in 2010, compared to 18,434,000 in 1950. The proportion of children below the age of 15 in 2010 was 41.5%, 55.2% was between 15 and 65 years of age, while 3.3% was 65 years or older.The average age is 25.1.
