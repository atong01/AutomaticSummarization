'''Jula''' ('''Dyula, Dioula''') is a [[Mande languages|Mande language]] spoken in [[Burkina Faso]], [[Ivory Coast]], and [[Mali]]. It is one of the [[Manding languages]], and is most closely related to [[Bambara language|Bambara]], being [[mutually intelligible]] with Bambara as well as [[Eastern Maninka language|Malinke]]. It is a [[trade language]] in [[West Africa]] and is spoken by millions of people, either as a first or second language. It is written in the [[Arabic script]] and the [[Latin script]], as well as in the indigenous [[N'Ko alphabet]].

A [[movie]] spoken in Dyula is [[Fanta Régina Nacro]]'s ''[[Night of Truth]]''.
