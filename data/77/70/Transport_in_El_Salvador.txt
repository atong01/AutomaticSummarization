The [[Central America]]n nation of [[El Salvador]] has [[transport]] links by [[Road transport|road]], [[Rail transport|rail]], [[Sea transport|sea]] and [[Air transport|air]].

El Salvador has over 10,000km of roads, and one passenger rail service. There are several [[seaports]] on the [[Pacific Ocean]], and two international [[airports]].

==Railways==
{{main|Rail transport in El Salvador}}
A weekday passenger service links [[San Salvador]] and [[Apopa]], a journey of 40 minutes.<ref>[http://www.fenadesal.gob.sv/tren_pasajeros.php Servicio de Tren de Pasajeros], FENADESAL, 2009</ref> Of a total of 602&nbsp;km [[Narrow gauge railway|narrow gauge]] ({{RailGauge|3ft}}) rail, much is abandoned. In November 2013 the government rail agency FENADESAL announced plans for development of four electrified railways serving San Salvador, Sitio del Niño ([[La Libertad Department, El Salvador|La Libertad]]), [[El Salvador International Airport]], [[La Unión, El Salvador|La Unión]], and the Honduran frontier.<ref>Jaime García, [http://www.elsalvador.com/mwedh/nota/nota_completa.asp?idCat=47673&idArt=8353978 ''La CEPA construirá nuevo sistema de ferrocarril en el país'' (CEPA will build a new rail system in the country")], elsalvador.com, 25 November 2013</ref>

==Highways==
[[Image:El-Salvador-Bus.jpg|thumb|300px|The bus running between Santa Ana and San Salvador.]]
*''total:'' 10,029&nbsp;km
*''paved:'' 1,986&nbsp;km (including 327&nbsp;km of [[Highway]]s)
*''unpaved:'' 8,043&nbsp;km (1999 est.)

The RN-21 (Bulevar Monseñor Romero) (East–West) is the very first freeway to be built in [[El Salvador]] and in [[Central America]]. The freeway passes the northern area of the city of Santa Tecla, La Libertad. It has a small portion serving [[Antiguo Cuscatlan]], La Libertad, and merges with the [[RN-5 (Autopista Comalapa)]] (East–West, Boulevard de Los Proceres/Autopista del Aeropuerto) in [[San Salvador]]. 

The total length of the RN-21 is {{convert|9.95|km|mi}} and is currently working as a traffic reliever in the metropolitan area. The RN-21 was named in honor of Monseñor Romero. The first phase of the highway was completed in 2009, and the second phase in November 2012.
