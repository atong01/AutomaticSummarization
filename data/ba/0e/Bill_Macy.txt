'''Bill Macy''' (born '''Wolf Martin Garber'''; May 18, 1922) is an [[United States|American]] television, film and stage actor, born in [[Revere, Massachusetts]], to Mollie (n√©e Friedopfer) and Michael Garber, a manufacturer.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.filmreference.com/film/93/Bill-Macy.html|title=Bill Macy Biography (1922-)|publisher=|accessdate=25 December 2014}}</ref>  He was raised in [[Brooklyn, New York]], and worked as a cab driver before pursuing acting.

Macy is best known for playing Walter Findlay, the long-suffering husband of the title character on the 1970s [[television]] [[situation comedy]] ''[[Maude (TV series)|Maude]]'', starring [[Beatrice Arthur]]. He also was an original cast member of the long-running theatrical revue ''[[Oh! Calcutta!]]'' He has made more than 70 appearances on film and television, including a memorable role as the co-inventor of the 'Opti-grab' in the 1979 [[Steve Martin]] comedy ''[[The Jerk]]'', and as the head television writer in ''[[My Favorite Year]]'' (1982). His other film credits have included roles in ''[[Death at Love House]]'' (1976), ''[[The Late Show (film)|The Late Show]]'' (1976), ''[[Serial (1980 film)|Serial]]'' (1980), ''[[Movers & Shakers]]'' (1985), ''[[Bad Medicine (film)|Bad Medicine]]'' (1985), ''[[Sibling Rivalry (film)|Sibling Rivalry]]'' (1990), ''[[The Doctor (1991 film)|The Doctor]]'' (1991), ''[[Me, Myself and I (film)|Me, Myself and I]]'' (1992), ''[[Analyze This]]'' (1999), ''[[Surviving Christmas]]'' (2004), and ''[[The Holiday]]'' (2006).

He appeared in the popular television movie ''Perry Mason & The Case Of The Murdered Madame'' (1987) as banker Richard Wilson. 

He appeared occasionally on ''[[Seinfeld]]'' as one of the residents of the Florida retirement community in which [[Jerry Seinfeld]]'s parents lived. He also appeared on the short-lived sitcom ''[[Back to You]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0534409/|title=Bill Macy|work=IMDb|accessdate=25 December 2014}}</ref> He made a guest appearance as a patient on ''[[Chicago Hope]]'' and as an aging gambler on the series ''[[Las Vegas (TV series)|Las Vegas]]''. In 2006 he made an appearance on ''[[My Name is Earl]]'' in the second-season episode, "[[Van Hickey]]" as an elderly patient in a nursing home who claims he "once tongue-kissed a Jamaican woman."
